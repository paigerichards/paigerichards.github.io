All Needs Transport

This application was built during my studies for the 'Term 3 Major Project', where we were to use React & Node JS.

The objective was to create something for a company that would be deemed 'useful', though it was to be understood by the company that this was a learning process and the expectation for it to be actually used isn't promised.

This project, was a group project. My group consisted of three other people, where we all collaborated but specialised in areas. For example, I worked mostly with React, alongside another member of the group and I did all the styling as per the wireframe, though making improvements.

What is All Needs Transport?
All Needs Transport is a delivery company that does contracts with Bunnings, Mac's Furniture & other related companies. It is currently based in Queensland, Victoria.

After a consultation with the company, via email. As a group we came to a conclusion of what each catergory of person would need within the app.

    As a Client...
  • I want to order a pick-up of freight to be transported
  • I want to organise a delivery for freight
  • I would like to have an overview of all active jobs
  • I would like access to complete history of all jobs, previous, current and future
  • I would like to be able to view the status of orders
  • I need to be able to alter job information, before the pick-up window starts
  • I need to be able to add extra jobs to my previous jobs
  • I need the ability to search through history with specific queries


    As a Driver...
  • I would like a list of all my jobs for the day
  • I would like to be able to mark a job as complete, for my own use
  • I would like to click on an address and be directed there


    As a Admin/Clerk...
  • I need to have access to all areas and aspects of the application
  • I must be the only user with permissions to add new users
  • I must be the only user with the ability to remove users
  • I must be the only user to alocate jobs to drivers
  • I must be the only user to alter jobs during the pick up window

From there, we discussed how layout, elements, intergrations may look like, how they may work, what pages they would be accessed from and began wireframing.

During the project we had some problems with the login, however they were resolved. We had major issues with the api and data sending through to the backend, at one point & this took a major toll, though we managed.

As a whole, we agreed having some sort of a discussion, every morning and a weekly roundup, to help minimise some confusion and problems that may arise. This created more communication within the group and was helpful in meeting our target.

We agreed to keep the design simple, and seperate what the client wanted into 'Must haves', 'Nice to have' & 'Least Important'. Doing this really focused our attention on the areas needed most to make the app perform as should.

It was a good experience to work on a big project as a group, to see how different minds work and see an appliction you worked on really come together.

Booking App

This idea for this particular booking app came from where I work. I work at a bar and bistro that has bands play every Friday & Saturday night.

The idea was to minimise the possiblity of entering data wrong, or not completing the full booking. For example currently, the booking system is an excel sheet. Feilds, such as payment, date of booking, the staff inital of who made the booking and ticket numbers issued are common areas to be left blank.

My solution to this was to make this app that requires the data entry before officially making the booking. I also wanted to do this for refunds and transfers as this is also evidently done incorrectly.
The first application of this would be adminastration side, with the possibility of people being able to book themselves through a mobile app.

The admin will be able to click the ‘booked out‘ button on the particular event to shut off bookings to that particular event and to reverse this, it would be password protected.

I’ve also allowed the admin to have a print version that will allow them to print out all the infomation for each booking, for seating purposes, ect.

In the event that the user application did happen, I’ve incorporated a notification popup, much like facebook to alert new bookings, where the admin would then ‘confirm‘ and the user would be sent an email addressing the confirmation of their booking. Upon confirmation is when the person will be charged the booking fee.
On the user application, I styled much like the API of Instagram, allowing for easy navigation and addresses the three main pages of the app.

I’ve incorporated a search bar for the admin as this is a major pain when sifting through an excel sheet when asked if so and so have booked in. This search would also filter through bands, which would be primarily the use for the user side app.

Above, you will see wireframe mockups of the application.
The desktop version is for adminstator and the mobile/ tablet version is for the user.

Inspire App

The Idea: I wanted to build an application that would benefit aspiring writers.

I wanted the users to be able to upload an excerpt of a novel they've written, intrest other users and have the possibility of income with users buying the full versions.

I wanted to expand this further to job opportunities. I decided that I would integrate a jobs section that would allow users to just click 'apply' and their information would be directly sent to the employer. From there the employer can view all the posts they've made, reviewed and liked. I also wanted to make it a bit more fun with a competition section.

I firstly restricted these to fields allowing only businesses that sign up being able to make job and competition posts but no novel posts.

Later, I agreed with feedback that this produced limitations within my app and I decided to give each user the ability to create any type of post, with the decision to sign up as a writer, business or critic was later scrapped.

Instagram has many great features, the app enables the user to search by; location, tags, usernames/actual names, follow users, contact them and tag them in posts, either one the user has made or in a comment. What appeals me to Instagram is its easy user experience, its simplistic and not complicated, quite straightforward. Facebook is VERY much simpler. Facebook allows the users to become friends which is much like the follow method on Instagram and connect option n LinkedIn. One thing that really stuck out about Facebook was its ability to have changeable newsfeeds; defaulted and customizable ones by the user.

After looking closely at these two apps, for my app, I'd like the user to be able to search specific for the page. For example if they are on the Job's page, the will only be given results with jobs relating to the title, experience level and location. I also want to implement a follow option, that will then give the user a 'following feed' with the root page displaying all users posts. This follow option will be found on their profile and will be located next to a contact button. Like Facebook, I would like to implement a Top Stories and Most Recent newsfeed filter.

I like how all applications use a simplistic icon navigation system at the bottom of the screen page on mobile. I would like to keep this idea consistent across all screen sizes with possibly having the bottom navigation, stick to the right hand side.

Having a look at LinkedIn, I really liked the interface of the profile page. I liked the idea of having a round picture with a frame including the person's full name, location, connections and beginning of a bio. I'm thinking of using a similar design.

    The user will be able to have...

  • A username, but with the ability to login with email or username
  • A profile picture
  • A record of their posts, reviews and likes accessible from their profile
  • Feed filters, eg; posts, jobs, competitions and top stories and most recent
  • Ability to view other users profiles
  • Ability to follow a user
  • Ability to contact user
  • Ability to apply for jobs and competitions
  • Search specific to the page
  • Ability to buy full versions of novels
  • Ability to like a post
  • Ability to review and rate a post
  • Ability to report inappropriate comments
  • Ability to see overall rate of a post
  • Biography


I'm more leaning to creating my app with a closely feel of Instagram for the as I feel like the design in place is more inviting than Facebook and Linkedin with its use of color.
